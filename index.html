Current work

Loopzero

An independent research program on how recursive systems — markets, models, recommenders — can quietly lose the ability to make progress before they break.

The framework is falsifiable and the directional evidence holds, but no detector — the standard methods or my own — has yet reached an accepted operating point under the locked test. So the framework stands; a working detector is still an open problem.

The throughline

A few years ago I started to suspect that the breakdowns around us — radicalization, polarization, outrage, eroding trust and attention spans — shared a common mechanism: digital systems feed on their own output until they consume themselves, and us with them. I spent a couple of years and a stack of books on it, and came out convinced there had to be something formal underneath — a claim we can actually test. That search is Loopzero.

The hardest, most valuable thing design teaches is to name the real problem. After a path through art history, design, film, and virtual reality, I think I’ve finally found the one worth naming.
