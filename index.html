Benchmarking Recursive-Collapse Warning Claims Under Matched False-Positive Control
David Mullett  ·  arXiv preprint
Loopzero is a claim-bounded benchmark framework for testing whether recursive failures show a directional telemetry pattern — rising gain (G), recursive persistence (p), and declining diversity (δ) — before overt collapse. The claim boundary is specified in Lean 4; detectors are then evaluated under a locked equal-false-positive contract on two frozen public benchmarks: a segmented markets family (Volmageddon 2018, COVID 2020) and a MovieLens-25M recommender replay. Digitized LLM training-loop trajectories (Shumailov et al., 2024) are included as a directional-consistency check. Where no detector clears that band, the result is reported in full.

Status

The claim boundary is specified in Lean 4. The bridge is tested on two frozen public benchmarks under a matched false-positive contract, with a directional-consistency check on published model-collapse trajectories (Shumailov et al., 2024). Recommender negative controls, cluster-robust dependence analysis, and additional domains remain open.

Context

Recursive feedback systems — algorithmic, financial, social — are widely studied, but their failure modes are usually described operationally rather than specified as formal objects. Loopzero treats one such failure mode as a bounded, falsifiable claim, so that what is asserted can be inspected, contested, and tested across domains.
